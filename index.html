Sorites Paradox 2014

I've always been interested in the relationship between a model of something and itself. In 2014 an astronomer explained to me that the world is not a sphere, as a sphere is only a three-dimensional shape described by one number.

Untitled 2015

I wondered if this is true of people and relationships. What does it take to create a model of a relationship? What does it take to represent something as nuanuced and perplexing as another person in relation to yourself?

First Mate 2015

My Kind 2015

Solipsistic anxiety that developed in 2009 forced me to seek refuge in the relationships of others by photographing love letters. The contents of the message are secondary to the knowledge that experience existed outside of myself.

Loveletter 4, 2015

It became clear that alienation was my premiere interest. More specifically, alienation as a result of language and translation. Language is messy, and vague at best. And for the longest time it was all we had to understand the past.

I became interested in the role of education in constructing versions of history. Ideas need to be simplified and condensed for travel, much like a file. This must effect the contents somehow, it must cause them to deteriorate in some way.

I'm now exploring the role of virtual reality in understanding things like presence and the authenticity of digital representation. By using photographs alone, 3D models of alien places can be created and navigated.
